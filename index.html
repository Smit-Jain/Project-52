Camel

  • There are two types of camels: One humped or “dromedary” camels and two humped Bactrian camels.
  • Camels have three sets of eyelids and two rows of eyelashes to keep sand out of their eyes.
  • Camels have thick lips which let them forage for thorny plants other animals can't eat.

Chameleon

  • Chameleon is a reptile found mostly in rainforests.
  • Chameleons change their body color according to their mood and surroundings.
  • Chameleons have a hard and crystal like body.

Cicada

  • Cicadas are insects which have prominent eyes set wide apart, short antennae, and membranous front wings.
  • Cicadas is of a group of sound-producing insects.
  • Cicadas are medium to large in size, ranging from 2 to 5 cm.

Panda

  • Giant pandas have a distinctive black and white coat, with black fur around their eyes and on their
    ears, muzzle, legs and shoulders.
  • At 5 months old, giant pandas learn how to climb.
  • They have an extended wrist bone that they use like a thumb to help them grip food.

Pigeon

  • Pigeons are incredibly complex and intelligent animals.
  • Pigeons are renowned for their outstanding navigational abilities.
  • Pigeons have excellent hearing abilities.
